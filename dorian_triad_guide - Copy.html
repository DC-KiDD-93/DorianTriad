Priority Framework
Health ROI
Where to invest time and attention for the highest return. These are estimated relative contributions — use them to prioritise effort, not as precise science.
The key insight: Sleep and nutrition together account for ~60% of your health and performance outcome. If training is optimised but sleep is poor, you are leaving the majority of your potential on the table. Fix the highest items first.

Foundation of everything — hormones, recovery, neuroplasticity, body composition. Non-negotiable.

Daily Protocol
Morning Routine
Already better than most. The three highest-leverage changes: eliminate the snooze cycle, move electrolytes to the moment of waking, reduce matcha to half to one teaspoon to avoid excess caffeine stacking.
Core principle: Delay first caffeine 60–90 minutes after waking. Cortisol peaks naturally at 30–45 min — let it work first, then layer caffeine on top. Already half-achieved by taking your espresso after 09:00.
07:00
Wake — no snooze
Alarm across the room. Snooze cycles cause fragmented sleep that deepens grogginess. Two 9-minute snoozes = 18 minutes of sleep inertia amplification, not recovery.
07:00
500ml water + electrolytes
Immediately on waking. Overnight dehydration is a primary driver of morning grogginess. Rehydrating before anything else accelerates adenosine clearance.
07:05
Open son's blinds
Natural light begins your circadian reset. On the school run — no sunglasses. 10 minutes of direct Tenerife morning sun is more powerful than most interventions.
07:45
Breakfast + matcha
Porridge bowl or eggs/bacon/avocado — target 40–50g protein. Reduce matcha to half to one tsp. Current one-tbsp dose = up to 280mg caffeine before your espresso.
08:15

Multivitamin, Omega-3 (3 capsules), Vitamin D3 — with food. Magnesium moves to bedtime, not morning.
08:30
School run by bike
10+ minutes direct morning sun — no sunglasses. Photoreceptors in the eyes are the primary circadian driver. This is your most powerful sleep-quality investment.
09:00+
First double espresso
Delayed ~90 minutes post-wake. Cortisol peaks naturally at 30–45 min — let it work before adding caffeine. This is already half-correct in your current routine.

Last caffeine of the day. 6-hour half-life means ~60mg still circulating at midnight — suppressing deep sleep without preventing sleep onset.
22:30

300–400mg, 30–60 minutes before sleep. Switch from oxide to glycinate. Sleep quality improvement compounds over weeks of consistent use.
Recovery Protocol
Rest & Recovery
Adaptation happens during recovery, not during training. The session is the stimulus — everything here is the response. Protecting recovery is as important as the training quality itself.
Sauna / Hot Bath
2–3× per week on rest days only
  • Do NOT use within 2 hours of training — blunts the acute inflammatory signal that drives adaptation
  • On rest days or the evening of training days, heat is beneficial — vasodilation, GH release, parasympathetic shift
  • Evidence for cardiovascular adaptation and longevity markers from regular sauna is robust
  • Mental reset value on rest days is real and hard to quantify — leverage it
Sleep
7–9 hours. The highest ROI item in the entire guide
  • The two nights after Hades are disproportionately important — deadlift sessions create the most CNS demand
  • Consistent 07:00 wake time regardless of when you sleep — the wake anchor sets the circadian clock
  • Dark, cool room. Magnesium glycinate 30–60 min before bed consistently
  • Don't train on fewer than 6 hours sleep if avoidable — anabolic signalling is meaningfully blunted
Breathing / Down-regulation
Post-session and as needed
  • Box breathing (4 sec in / 4 hold / 4 out / 4 hold) for 5 minutes post-heavy session shifts sympathetic to parasympathetic
  • Physiological sigh (double inhale through nose, long exhale through mouth) is the fastest known acute stress reducer
  • Nasal breathing during walking and low-intensity movement improves CO2 tolerance and sleep quality over time
  • 5-minute walk post-session is not optional — it actively assists recovery and down-regulation
Hydration
2.5–3L daily minimum. More on training days and hot days
  • 500ml with electrolytes immediately on waking — before coffee, before food
  • Electrolytes during training and on hot Tenerife days — 650mg sodium per serve is well-dosed
  • Pale yellow urine is the target — reliable real-time indicator
  • Thirst signal lags actual dehydration by a meaningful margin — don't rely on it alone
Cold Exposure
Not recommended post-training
  • Cold immersion immediately post-training significantly blunts hypertrophic adaptation — stronger evidence than heat
  • If you use cold exposure, minimum 4 hours after training, or on rest days only
  • No strong rationale for cold exposure given current goals, warm climate and no endurance sport component
  • Contrast therapy (hot then cold) on rest days is acceptable if desired for general recovery feel
Active Recovery
Rest days are not sedentary days
  • Walking, light cycling, swimming — low intensity movement maintains blood flow without adding stress
  • Tenerife offers excellent outdoor activity — leverage the climate and terrain on rest days
  • Full ROM joint movement (arm circles, hip circles, light stretching) maintains mobility without adding soreness
  • Progressive overload is only productive because of recovery periods — protect them as seriously as training days
Long Game
Lifestyle Factors
Supporting contributors that compound powerfully over years. Lower individual ROI weight than sleep or training, but largely free to implement and sustainable indefinitely.
Gut Health
  • Target 30+ different plant foods per week — diversity is the primary microbiome health metric
  • Daily fermented foods: Greek yogurt, kefir if available — live cultures matter for microbial diversity
  • Fibre minimum 30g daily — oats, legumes, vegetables, fruit, whole grains
  • Minimise ultra-processed food, refined sugar and artificial sweeteners — all documented microbiome disruptors
Flexibility & ROM
  • Full ROM on every exercise in the programme is more valuable than dedicated stretching sessions — this is where the flexibility work happens
  • Post-session stretching (chest, hip flexors, hamstrings) compounds significantly over months for just 5 minutes per day
  • Thoracic mobility is the most limiting factor for most pressing athletes — extensions over a bench or foam roller daily
  • Deep squat and full hip hinge patterns with true ROM prevent restriction that accumulates from training without mobility work
Sunlight & Vitamin D
  • Morning sunlight without sunglasses within 60 minutes of waking — the most powerful free circadian intervention
  • Despite living in Tenerife, supplement 2500 IU D3 daily — years in Ireland likely created a deficiency not yet corrected
  • Mid-day Tenerife sun is intense — high SPF at peak hours is sensible. Low-angle morning sun has lower UV risk
  • D3 plays documented roles in testosterone production, muscle function, immune regulation and mood stabilisation
Caffeine Strategy
  • Total daily: half-1 tsp matcha + 2 double espressos = 190–320mg — within range but upper end
  • Delay first caffeine 60–90 min after waking — let natural cortisol peak work first
  • Last caffeine by 12:00 noon — 6-hour half-life means meaningful residual at midnight
  • Periodic caffeine breaks (5–7 days every 6–8 weeks) restore full sensitivity — tolerance builds within a week
Stress & Mental Health
  • Chronic stress elevates cortisol which directly suppresses testosterone and growth hormone — the hormones you need
  • The consistency built this year is itself a stress-management tool — routine creates psychological stability
  • Training from identity and enjoyment is more durable than training from feeling too skinny — both work, one compounds better over 20 years
  • Sleep, training, good food and morning sunlight are evidence-based mood regulators — the lifestyle IS the mental health protocol
Cardiovascular Health
  • Your training already provides significant cardiovascular stimulus through compound movements and density of work
  • Walking daily — particularly in Tenerife terrain — provides Zone 2 cardiovascular work that compounds over time
  • Nasal breathing during low-intensity activity is the simplest cardiovascular health habit available
  • Sauna 2–3x weekly adds meaningful cardiovascular adaptation independent of exercise — VO2max and heart rate variability both improve
The integrated picture: Training is intelligent, diet is solid, supplements are well-chosen, lifestyle is well-structured. You are more consistent than you have ever been and heavier than you have ever been while staying lean. The one variable with the greatest remaining upside is sleep quality — specifically the depth of sleep in the 48 hours following Hades. Protect those nights above all others.
